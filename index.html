Foundation Models for Dynamical Systems · Representation Learning
Ph.D. Candidate · Interdisciplinary Program in Artificial Intelligence
Seoul National University
Email GitHub LinkedIn CV

I study how machine learning can uncover hidden structure in high-dimensional, heterogeneous observations of complex dynamical systems. Real-world data are rarely clean signals—they are mixtures of stochastic processes, measurement artifacts, and multi-scale dynamics that obscure the mechanisms generating them. My research explores how this intrinsic structure can be discovered and explicitly embedded into model architectures to build robust foundation models. Neural signals serve as a demanding testbed, with broader implications for foundation models of dynamical systems across domains such as video and multimodal sensory data.

Selected Research
POYO-CAP

Cell-Type-Aware Pretraining for Neural Decoding

Data-centric pretraining strategy accounting for neuron-level statistical heterogeneity in calcium imaging recordings. Prioritizes statistically predictable neurons to stabilize training.

SSIM 0.593 (+13%) on visual reconstruction · 4×V100
INCAMA

Whole-Brain Causal Connectivity under Indirect Observation

Physics-aware ML framework reconstructing latent neural activity from indirect measurements (fMRI/EEG) before performing directed causal discovery at brain-network scale.

~309% higher F1 in causal pathway recovery vs. DL baselines
MBBN

Frequency-Specific Multi-Band Attention for fMRI

Neuroscience-informed transformer modeling multi-scale temporal structure in brain signals, enabling interpretable frequency-dependent connectivity on large clinical datasets.

AUROC +41% on ASD classification · 49k+ subjects
PIMSM

Physics-Informed Multi-Scale State Space Model

Physics-informed multi-scale state-space architecture for robust sequence modeling under acquisition shifts. Anchors discretization dynamics to acquisition time units and spectrum-derived temporal scales, improving latent alignment under temporal resolution changes.

CKA ↑ up to 45% vs. CNN baseline · 1% supervision on HCP motor decoding
Object-LVDM

Object-Centric Loss for Text-to-Video Generation

Object-centric loss for text-to-video generation that improves temporal consistency by supervising persistent objects rather than frame-wise differences. Lightweight Slot Attention plug-in identifies object slots and enforces object-level temporal alignment across frames.

Improved temporal consistency in text-conditioned video generation
